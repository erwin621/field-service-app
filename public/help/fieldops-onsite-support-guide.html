Getting started with FieldOps

Getting In

Signing In

You don't create your own account — your supervisor or admin sets it up for you. All you need is your name and a PIN.

How to sign in

  1. Open the web address your supervisor gave you, in your phone's browser.
  2. If you see a choice of portals, choose Technician (use "Back to portal" if you land on the wrong screen).
  3. Tap Your Name and find yourself in the list.
  4. Enter your PIN Code and tap Sign In to Tech Portal.
First time signing in? You'll be asked to set your own PIN before you can continue. Enter the temporary PIN you were given as your Current PIN, then choose a new 4–6 digit PIN and type it twice to confirm. This only happens once — or again if an admin ever resets your PIN.
Forgot your PIN? You can't reset it yourself — ask your supervisor or admin to reset it. You'll set a new one the next time you sign in. For quicker access next time, add the page to your phone's Home Screen from your browser's menu, so it opens like a regular app.
2
The Basics

Get Oriented

Everything in the app lives behind four buttons at the bottom of the screen.

OPEN
Jobs nobody has claimed yet, sorted closest to you first.
TASKS
Your own jobs — My Jobs (in progress), Completed, and Cancelled.
TOOLS
Camera, Document Scan, My Route, Navigator, Timer, Income, Settings.
PROFILE
Your details, the On/Off Duty switch, notifications, help, and sign out.

Going On Duty / Off Duty

  1. Go to Profile and flip the Availability switch to On Duty before you start work — this is what lets you see and reserve jobs.
  2. Switch to Off Duty at the end of your shift, or on a break, so nothing new gets assigned to you.
  3. If you still have sites reserved when you go Off Duty, the app will ask whether to keep them reserved for later or release them for other technicians.
3
Getting Work

Find a Job & Lock It In

Browsing a job doesn't start it — you first reserve it so nobody else can take it, then claim it when you're ready to work (Stop 4).

Reserve a single site

  1. On the Open tab, browse jobs near you — closest ones appear first once you allow location access.
  2. Tap the filter icon to narrow the list to one area/city, if you like.
  3. Tap a job card to open its full details — address, map, and distance.
  4. If it's free, slide the bar at the bottom (Slide to Reserve) all the way across. This locks the site to you and takes you straight to My Route.
A job marked "Reserved by [name]" is already locked by someone else. You'll need to wait for them to release it or complete it.

Reserve a whole area at once

Heading to a whole neighborhood for the day? Go to Tools → My Route → Start a Route, pick a city or area, and every open job there gets reserved for you in one go — no need to reserve each one individually.

4
On Site

Claim It, Then Work It

Reserving locks a site to you. Claiming is the separate step that actually starts the job and moves it into your Tasks.

Claim a reserved site

  1. Open Tools → My Route. Your reserved sites are listed in a suggested visiting order, closest first.
  2. Find the site you're at (or about to visit) and tap Claim.
  3. It moves straight to Tasks → My Jobs, ready for you to work on.

Not doing a site after all? Tap Remove to drop just that one, or Release Route to give up everything you've reserved.

Reserved sites aren't held forever — if there's no activity on your route for 4 hours, the reservation releases automatically so others can pick it up.

Complete the Guided Troubleshooting checklist

  1. Open the job in My Jobs and start the troubleshooting checklist ("Need Help?").
  2. Go through each check in order and mark it Passed or Failed.
  3. If a check fails, attach a photo and a short note about what you found — this is required before you can move on.
  4. Some failed checks stop the checklist early, since later checks depend on that one — you'll see "Diagnostic Stopped." Tap Override to continue anyway, or fix the issue first.
  5. At the end, review the summary and confirm it — this is what unlocks Submit & Complete on the job.
Not sure what a check is asking? Profile → Help & Support has the entire checklist, searchable — worth a look before you even leave for the site.
Camera
From a job, tap the camera icon (or Tools → Camera). Photos are auto-stamped with date, time, GPS, and site ID. Flip the camera, use flash if it's dark, or hold to record video with sound. You'll always see a preview — retake or attach it.
Document Scan
Tools → Document Scan for signed forms or receipts. The app finds the page edges for you — drag the corners if needed, pick a cleaner look, add more pages, then Save. Multi-page scans save as one PDF and attach straight to the job.
5
Wrapping Up

Submit or Cancel

Every job ends one of two ways: completed with proof, or cancelled with a reason.

Submit a completed job

  1. In My Jobs, attach at least one photo or video — from the camera or your phone's gallery (up to 5 files).
  2. Add a note if it's useful.
  3. Tap Submit & Complete. This stays greyed out until the troubleshooting checklist (Stop 4) is finished.

Cancel a job you can't complete

  1. Open the job and tap Cancel.
  2. Type a short reason — this is required.
  3. Confirm. It moves to your Cancelled list under Tasks.

Getting Around

Plan Your Route

Once you've claimed more than one job, let the app work out the best order to visit them.

Navigator

  1. Go to Tools → Navigator.
  2. Tap Optimize Route — it works out the best order for your claimed jobs and draws it on a map.
  3. Filter by Today, High Priority, or one area, and switch on Avoid Tolls if that matters to you.
  4. Tap Start to open turn-by-turn directions in Google Maps or Waze.

You can also keep checking Tools → My Route through the day — it shows what's left, reorders by distance automatically, and lets you add more sites or release ones you won't get to today.

7
Staying On Top Of It

Track Your Time & Earnings

Both of these update automatically — there's nothing to start or fill in yourself.

Timer
Tools → Timer. Tracks automatically from the moment you claim a job until you submit it, and keeps a history of past jobs too.
Income Overview
Tools → Income Overview. Your earnings from completed jobs, filterable by Today / Week / Month or a custom date range, with a chart and CSV/Excel export.
8
Your Account

Profile & Getting Help

Everything about you — and how to reach someone when the app can't answer it.

Personal Info

Profile → Personal Info — add your nickname, birth date, address, and a profile photo. Your photo replaces your initial in the header, so make sure it clearly shows your face.

Telegram Notifications (optional)

Want your own completed-job proofs sent straight to you on Telegram?

  1. Go to Profile → Preferences → Telegram Settings.
  2. Create a bot using Telegram's @BotFather, then get your Bot Token and Chat ID.
  3. Paste both values in, tap Send Test Message to check it works, then Save Settings.

Once saved, the values are hidden for your security — tap the pencil icon to change them later.

Camera Settings
Tools → Settings. Choose what shows on your photos (date/time, GPS, site ID) and how it looks (position, size, color).
Help & Support
Profile → Help & Support. A searchable copy of the full troubleshooting checklist — handy to check any time.
Done for the day? Profile → Sign Out, at the very bottom.

Terms Used In The App

Glossary

Job / Ticket
One site that needs a visit. Has its own code, like FO-20260718-000123.
Locality
The city or area a site is in — used for filtering, and for reserving a whole area at once.
On Duty / Off Duty
Whether you're currently available to see and reserve new jobs.
Reserve / Reservation
Locking a site to yourself before you claim it, so nobody else can take it.
Claim
Officially starting a job — moves it into My Jobs so you can work it.
Route
The group of sites you've reserved for the day, shown in Tools → My Route.
Proof
The photo, video, or scanned document you attach when you finish a job.
Guided Troubleshooting
The step-by-step checklist you complete on site before you can submit.
PIN
Your personal 4–6 digit sign-in code — not one you pick yourself the very first time.
When Something Looks Off

Frequently Asked Questions

I don't see any jobs on the Open tab
Make sure you're On Duty (Profile → Availability). Check whether a locality filter is turned on — clear it if so. Also make sure you've allowed the app to use your location.
The Submit & Complete button is greyed out
Finish the Guided Troubleshooting checklist on that job first. The button unlocks once you review and confirm the summary at the end of the checklist.
It says "Cannot reach server"
Check your internet connection and try again. Your entries usually aren't lost — just retry the action.
I forgot my PIN
You can't reset it yourself. Ask your supervisor or admin to reset it for you — you'll be asked to choose a new PIN the next time you sign in.
Someone else already reserved the job I wanted
Jobs are first-come, first-served. If you want a better chance at a whole area, use My Route to reserve it early in the day.
My reservation disappeared
Reservations release automatically after 4 hours with no activity, so other technicians can pick them up. Open My Route and reserve again if the site is still open.
Can I change my Telegram Bot Token after saving it?
Yes — go to Profile → Preferences, tap the pencil icon next to Telegram Settings, and enter the new values.
What are the "Inventory" and "Alerts" tiles in Tools?
Those are marked "Coming Soon" — they're not available to use yet.
